Poems by Ingrid Iverson.

The Poverty Rate

The poverty rate

Below eye level

Where no one looks

What about those

Above the poverty rate

But still bellow eye level?

The poverty rate

Considers

The desperate few

Doesn’t consider

The struggling many

Consider them

It rises

And rises

And rises

Until

You must notice

Until

It rises

And rises

And rises

To eye level




Poverty is:

Not having

- Food

- Clothes

- Shelter

- Money

Not being

- The kind of person who gifts something to someone else

- The kind of person who obtains a higher education

- The kind of person who affords luxuries

- The kind of person who has new clothes, soap, and hot water

- The kind of person who walks into a store and buys something

- The kind of person who doesn’t worry 24/7 about what they will go without

Not knowing

- What to do

- How to get out

- Who will help

- Whether there is any hope left




Poems by Anika Lindberg

Unsatisfied Needs

Oh, the jab in my heart

When passing the restaurant

Such fancy foods

A gem to my hungry stomach


I look to my mom

She shakes her head, no

I barely have any

We don’t have enough


Skipping to the store

Hoping for a meal

But only four person meals

For our family of 9


I, a twelve-year-old

The eldest of many

Naturally give it

To my younger siblings


But I forgo my growth

For their health

Sometimes

You can’t sacrifice




Nobody’s Help

Nobody

I repeat

Nobody helped me


The suffering I endured left me

Scarred

Nobody helped me out

And kept me

From passing this fate


Not a single person offered me

A small chance

To sustain the optimism

That since has left me


I now love

And feel

But nobody helped me

Save the life of

Another poor child

Doomed to poverty
